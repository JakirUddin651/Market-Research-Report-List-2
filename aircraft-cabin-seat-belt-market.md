<p><p>The aircraft cabin seat belt market primarily consists of three types: two-point, three-point, and four-point seatbelts. Two-point seatbelts are simple designs that secure passengers across the lap. Three-point seatbelts offer added safety by including an upper shoulder harness, distributing impact forces more effectively. Four-point seatbelts provide maximum security, anchoring passengers at four points for enhanced protection during turbulence or crashes. Each type balances comfort, safety regulations, and operational requirements to ensure passenger security during flights.</p></p>
