<p><p>The FRAM storage market is primarily divided into two types: Serial FRAM and Parallel FRAM. Serial FRAM utilizes a single data line to transmit information, making it ideal for applications requiring simplicity and space efficiency. In contrast, Parallel FRAM employs multiple data lines, allowing for faster data transfer rates suitable for high-performance applications. Both types cater to different market needs, with Serial FRAM often used in smaller devices and Parallel FRAM in more complex systems that demand speed and efficiency.</p></p>
