<p><p>Water quality sensors in agriculture play a crucial role in monitoring the health of irrigation water. Turbidity sensors measure particulate matter, indicating water clarity and potential contamination. Temperature sensors track water temperature, influencing crop growth and microbial activity. pH sensors assess acidity or alkalinity, essential for nutrient availability. Conductivity sensors gauge the concentration of dissolved salts, affecting plant health. Dissolved oxygen sensors monitor oxygen levels, vital for aquatic life. Collectively, these sensors enhance sustainable farming practices and optimize water management.</p></p>
