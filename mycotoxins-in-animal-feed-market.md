<p><p>Mycotoxins in animal feed can be categorized into two main market types: Type I and Type II. Type I mycotoxins, such as aflatoxins and fumonisins, are often produced by fungi in specific environmental conditions, posing significant health risks to livestock and affecting food safety. Type II mycotoxins, including trichothecenes and zearalenone, are linked to more chronic effects on animal health and productivity. Understanding these types helps in developing effective management strategies to mitigate their impact in the animal feed industry.</p></p>
