<p><ul><li>Microfiltration</li><li>Ultrafiltration</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Alumina tubular ceramic membranes are categorized into microfiltration and ultrafiltration types based on pore size and application. Microfiltration membranes feature larger pore sizes, effectively removing suspended solids and bacteria, making them suitable for water treatment and food processing. In contrast, ultrafiltration membranes have smaller pores, enabling the separation of macromolecules, proteins, and colloids, thus finding applications in pharmaceuticals and biotechnology. Both types utilize the durability and chemical resistance of alumina ceramics, enhancing filtration efficiency and longevity.</p></p>
